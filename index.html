The Wise Dreams · Triportation

The barrier wasn't age: making a VR travel experience easier to understand

The team's first read was that age explained the difficulty. Research showed people needed familiar reference points to get started, orient themselves and move forward in VR — regardless of age.

  • Contextual observation
  • Usability testing
  • VR/AR
Result We redesigned around a single entry point — choosing a destination — organized on a 3D table, with content revealed progressively instead of all at once.
View research Pujobaixo

From a WhatsApp group to a local network for sharing rides

Pujobaixo grew out of a WhatsApp group that already coordinated rides between the Berguedà and Barcelona. The research turned that informal coordination into a product without losing what made it work.

  • Contextual observation
  • Conversation analysis
  • Service modeling
Result The product centralized active trips, separated supply from demand, and kept WhatsApp for final coordination — preserving the flexibility that made the community work.
View research Agile Monkeys

The problem wasn't discovery. It was shipping.

Agile Monkeys was exploring an AI recommendation engine for vinyl buyers. Research redirected the concept toward a more relevant purchasing problem.

  • Semi-structured interviews
  • Concept validation
  • Product strategy
Result The team reconsidered the recommendation engine and explored order bundling and seller trust instead.

About me

Research before you build

My background in Fine Arts trained me to observe behavior critically, spot patterns and question assumptions before designing anything. That instinct evolved into user research applied to travel, mobility, marketplace and immersive-experience products.

I've worked at the intersection of user needs, product decisions and technical feasibility. My experience as a Product Owner lets me turn findings into priorities, align teams, and define what evidence we need to reduce uncertainty, avoid premature solutions and build with purpose.
