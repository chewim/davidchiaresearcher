Agile Monkeys

The problem wasn't discovery. It was shipping.

Research redirected an AI recommendation concept toward a more relevant purchasing problem.

Role
UX Researcher — Freelance
Duration
Mar — Apr 2024
Methods
Semi-structured interviews
Sector
E-commerce · Music

Agile Monkeys was exploring an AI-powered recommendation engine for vinyl buyers. Before investing further in the solution, we investigated whether discovering new records was actually the problem users needed solved.

Research approach

I conducted four semi-structured interviews with people closely connected to the music ecosystem, including profiles related to music production, vinyl collecting and specialised purchasing.

The objective was exploratory: to understand how experienced users discovered, evaluated and purchased records, and to identify the main barriers throughout that process.

The key insight

Participants already had established ways of discovering music through artists, labels, record stores and specialised communities.

The main friction appeared later in the journey: shipping costs made individual purchases difficult to justify.

Users responded by postponing purchases, saving records or searching for a single trusted seller offering several items they wanted.

The opportunity was therefore not simply to recommend more records.

It was to help buyers find a reliable seller whose catalogue allowed them to group several desired records into one order.

Product decision

The research helped the team reconsider the initial recommendation engine and explore a different product direction:

  • Prioritising catalogue and seller matching.
  • Supporting the bundling of several records into one purchase.
  • Making seller trust and reliability more visible.
  • Validating the concept before committing further development resources.

Research impact

The research challenged a technology-led assumption and revealed a problem connected to users' real purchasing behaviour.

Rather than asking how AI could recommend more records, the team began asking how the product could make buying them more viable.
