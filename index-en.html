The Wise Dreams · Triportation

The barrier wasn't age: bringing virtual reality closer to travelers over 40

We started out assuming age was the main barrier to using Triportation in VR. The research showed the real problem was a lack of familiar reference points, guidance and control.

  • Exploratory interviews
  • Evaluative testing
  • VR/AR
Result Segmenting by behavior — not age — redirected the redesign toward progressive onboarding, guided navigation and a web/mobile-to-VR transition.
View research Pujobaixo

From the intention to carpool to actually posting a trip

Almost everyone said they'd carpool between the Berguedà and Barcelona. The research revealed why so few actually posted a trip.

  • Interviews
  • Journey Map
  • Usability testing
Result The redesigned posting flow visualizes the savings, explains the process in 3 steps and gives drivers control over who rides with them.
View research Agile Monkeys

The problem wasn't finding more records. It was paying less to receive them.

The research revealed that vinyl buyers didn't need more recommendations — they needed a trustworthy seller to bundle their order with and cut down on shipping.

  • Interviews
  • Thematic analysis
  • Product strategy
Result The evidence made it possible to rethink the product's initial direction before building a solution with little real impact.

About me

Research before you build

My background in Fine Arts trained me to observe behavior critically, spot patterns and question assumptions before designing anything. That instinct evolved into user research applied to travel, mobility, marketplace and immersive-experience products.

I've worked at the intersection of user needs, product decisions and technical feasibility. My experience as a Product Owner lets me turn findings into priorities, align teams, and define what evidence we need to reduce uncertainty, avoid premature solutions and build with purpose.
